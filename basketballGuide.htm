Basketball

Baskeball has been played since December 21, 1891, beginning in Springfield, Massachusetts and evolving into a sport played all across the United States, and more recently, all across the world. Now, High Schoolers, like myself, across the country love to play it. An estimated 939,836 student athletes, including both boys and girls play the sport.

Rules

  • High level basketball games are usually either played in quarters of 12 minutes(NBA) and in halves of 20 minutes(NCAA)
  • In between the halves of these games, each team recieves 15 minutes as a halftime break
  • If the score at the end of regulation(the end of the four 12 minute quarters or two 20 minute halves) is the same, the game will go into overtime, where an additional 5 minutes is played and until one of the teams scores higher than the other by the end of the overtime, additional overtimes will begin until one of the teams wins
  • Each team has to shoot the ball within each possession at least once within 24 seconds but if you shoot and hit the rim the clock resets
  • After a dribble, a player can take two steps before the player must dribble again. If he doesn't, it is a travel, which is a foul
  • Fouls generally turn the ball over but there are shooting fouls where if you were fouled within the three point line, you get two free throws but if you were fouled outside the three point line you get to take three free throws
  • The referees are the ones who officiate the games and they call whether there was a foul or not.

NBA

The NBA is the most elite level of basketball. It is every young basketball player's dream to make it to the NBA. The NBA is responsible for popularizing some of pop cultures most notable names: Michael Jordan, Kobe Bryant, LeBron James, and Allen Iverson. Fortunately for data analysts, statistics in the NBA are well documented, so when arguing over who is the GOAT, which is a very popular topic to argue about amoung basketball fans, there can be some factual basis rather than pure bias.
